✅ A first taste

Code: blog/01-embeddings/hello_embeddings.py

Four sentences — two about gardening, two about finance. The model (sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2, 384-d) never sees the topic labels.

  0.622   'I love plants'              vs  'Gardening is fun'             ← gardening
  0.149   'The stock market crashed'   vs  'Bonds and treasury yields'    ← finance
  0.021   'I love plants'              vs  'The stock market crashed'
  0.016   'I love plants'              vs  'Bonds and treasury yields'
  0.015   'Gardening is fun'           vs  'The stock market crashed'
  0.005   'Gardening is fun'           vs  'Bonds and treasury yields'

Cross-pairs score 30× to 120× lower than the same-topic pairs.

The gardening pair (0.62) outscores the finance pair (0.15) — both topical neighbors, but “I love plants” and “Gardening is fun” are closer in sentiment-and-meaning than “stock market crashed” (a panic event) and “bonds and treasury yields” (fixed-income instruments). Embeddings reflect meaning, not just topic labels.

✅ Brute-force search on 5,000 news passages

Code: blog/01-embeddings/search_corpus.py

Corpus: 5,000 passages from fancyzhx/ag_news. Embeddings cached to disk after the first run. Top-k via one matmul per query against the L2-normalized corpus matrix.

Sample query: “tennis player wins grand slam”. Top result:

Roddick escapes, but not Federer. ATHENS, Greece. Andy Roddick #39;s biggest obstacle to winning the gold medal was Roger Federer…

The phrase “grand slam” does not appear in the passage. The embedding placed it as the closest match because Roddick + Federer + Olympics + gold medal sits at the right point in tennis-meaning-space.

Another query: “earnings report from a tech company”. The third match was Limited Brands — a clothing retailer with earnings reports. The embedding weighted earnings report heavily and partially missed tech company; when a query bundles multiple concepts, the embedding averages them and one concept can dominate. This is exactly why production RAG adds reranking, query rewriting, and hybrid sparse+dense search.

Steady-state search latency at N=5,000: ~0.7 ms per query (single CPU core).

✅ Latency scaling — when brute force breaks

Code: blog/01-embeddings/latency_scaling.py

Measured: K=10, 50 queries per N, 384-d vectors, single CPU core (Intel i5-9300H), L2-normalized matmul.

N mean (ms) p95 (ms)
100 0.017 0.021
500 0.033 0.036
1,000 0.052 0.067
2,500 0.279 0.440
5,000 0.605 0.857

Past N ≈ 1,000 the curve is linear: ~121 ns per document. Linear extrapolation:

Latency budget Brute-force ceiling
10 ms ~83,000 docs
100 ms ~830,000 docs
1 s ~8.3 million docs

For reference: English Wikipedia is ≈ 6 million articles, MS MARCO is ≈ 8.8 million passages. Brute force can’t hit a 10 ms production budget on either; HNSW, IVF, and PQ exist to climb past this wall.
